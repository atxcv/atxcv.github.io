Context & Goal

In SAP C4C, Relationship Intelligence (RI) surfaces engagement insights when the account has valid website/domain data. I supported the effort to normalize account websites and contact domains so more accounts could transition from RI No → RI Yes.

RI semantics (public-safe)
RI Yes Feature available → engagement visible.
RI No Feature not available → no engagement shown.
After changes, some accounts moved from RI No to RI Yes.

Scope & Constraints

  • Top 35 accounts prioritized
  • ~4,000 accounts updated so far
  • Excel as primary tool (no code)
  • Confidential data not shared

What I Did

  • Cleaned & standardized websites/domains for accounts and contacts.
  • Summarized progress in Excel using VLOOKUP, filters, conditionals, and pivot tables.
  • Reviewed allowlisted domains and tracked accounts auto-modified by Siebel users to re-validate.
  • Prepared files and documentation for SAP support cases.

How I Worked

  • Excel pipelines (sheets for input, checks, pivots, and summary).
  • Clear before/after comparison of RI availability per account group.
  • Daily/weekly reporting and concise, reproducible steps.
  • Global collaboration in agile routines.

Impact (Illustrative, Anonymized)

Below is a safe, qualitative summary you can keep even if exact figures are confidential:

  • Increased number of accounts with RI Yes after standardization, enabling new engagement insights.
  • Improved data availability and reliability for decision-makers.
  • Reduced rework by documenting a repeatable Excel review flow (checklists + pivots).
